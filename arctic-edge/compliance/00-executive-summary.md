# Executive Summary

**Audience:** CEO, President, CCO, General Counsel.
**Length:** One page. Read time: 3 minutes.

## The opportunity

Brokerage firms and RIAs are already using general-purpose AI chatbots to
draft Reg BI notes, client communications, and trade rationales. The SEC and
FINRA have both publicly flagged this practice. The 2024 FINRA Regulatory
Notice 24-09 ("AI in the Securities Industry") and the SEC's December 2024
Examination Priorities both warn that firms remain responsible for the work
product their advisors generate, regardless of how it was drafted.

The practice today is, in substantial part, **post-hoc justification**: the
trade is placed first, then the AI is asked to make it sound defensible. Both
regulators see this. Tighter rules are coming.

## Arctic Edge is the inverse of post-hoc justification

Arctic Edge is built on a single architectural principle:

> The advisor cannot generate documentation for a recommendation
> until the elements that make the recommendation reasonable are
> already on the record.

The IPS Module captures objectives, constraints, and risk capacity. The Trade
Thesis Library captures the variant perception, catalyst, time horizon, and
exit condition **before** the trade. The Audit Trail records origin
(solicited vs. unsolicited) and alternatives considered. The Portfolio Risk
Analyzer produces deterministic risk metrics with disclosed method
assumptions. Only after those records exist can the Reg BI Note Generator
assemble a note, and the Note Completeness Auditor will affirmatively flag
anything missing.

This is a constraint, not a convenience. It produces the record the rules
require because the workflow makes the record impossible to skip.

## Why this reduces, rather than increases, regulatory risk

| Risk under status quo | How Arctic Edge addresses it |
|---|---|
| Notes hand-written or AI-drafted after the trade, missing elements | Affirmative checklist (Module 7) flags every Reg BI element |
| Inconsistent documentation across advisors | Single template (`reg-bi-note/v1`), versioned and CCO-signed |
| No record of alternatives considered | Required field in Module 6, captured in Module 5 (Audit Trail) |
| Solicited/unsolicited tagging unreliable | Required at point of capture; append-only |
| Risk language varies per advisor | Module 3 produces deterministic metrics; Module 2 prevents collapse to a single score |
| Concentration risk undisclosed | Module 9 surfaces look-through single-name exposure |
| Books-and-records gaps at exam | Every output is a versioned, exportable JSON artifact |

## What we are asking the firm to do

1. Have the CCO and General Counsel review this package.
2. Tailor the two regulated templates (`reg-bi-note/v1`, `reg-bi-checklist/v1`)
   to the firm's product mix, share classes, and conflict inventory.
3. Execute the sign-off (`11-sign-off-template.md`).
4. Run the 90-day pilot (`08-pilot-rollout.md`) with a small advisor team.
5. Move to firm-wide adoption.

## Why now

Three converging pressures:

1. **Regulatory tightening is signaled.** The SEC and FINRA have both
   published warnings. New rulemaking on AI use in the industry is in the
   public conversation.
2. **First-mover advantage on standards.** Whichever tools are in use at
   reputable firms when the rules are finalized become the de facto reference
   implementations. Firms that adopted SMA performance composites pre-GIPS
   were grandfathered into the standard; firms that did not had to retool.
3. **Cost of exam findings is rising.** SEC and FINRA fines for inadequate
   Reg BI documentation have moved from cease-and-desist to monetary
   sanctions during the 2024–2025 cycle.

## What Arctic Edge is not

It is not a black-box AI scoring engine. There are no hidden risk scores. The
Four-Quadrant Risk Framework explicitly blocks collapse to a single number.
Every metric the Portfolio Risk Analyzer produces has its method assumptions
shown in the UI alongside the result. The advisor's judgment is what gets
documented; the system does not override it, and Module 7 never blocks the
advisor from filing a note.

## Bottom line

Status quo means the firm carries the risk of inconsistent, post-hoc
documentation generated by tools the regulator has publicly warned against.
Arctic Edge converts the same workflow into a record that satisfies the rules
because the elements were captured in the right order. The pilot can begin
within 30 days of executed sign-off.
