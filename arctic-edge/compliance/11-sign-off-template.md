## 2. Scope of Sign-Off

The undersigned Chief Compliance Officer ("CCO") of the firm identified
above, having reviewed the Arctic Edge compliance package, hereby
approves the following for use in the firm's Reg BI compliance program,
Adviser Act compliance program (if applicable), and FINRA supervisory
program (if applicable):

### 2a. Regulated templates

The undersigned approves the following Arctic Edge templates, **as
tailored by the firm and attached to this sign-off as Exhibits A and B**:

- ☐ **Exhibit A**: `reg-bi-note/v1` — Reg BI Note Generator template
  (tailored).
- ☐ **Exhibit B**: `reg-bi-checklist/v1` — Note Completeness Auditor
  checklist element list and cue phrases (tailored).

The exhibits supersede the generic templates at
`templates/reg-bi-note-v1.md` and `templates/reg-bi-checklist-v1.md`.

### 2b. Operational data contracts

The undersigned approves the use of the following non-regulated data
contracts:

- ☐ `ips-record/v1`
- ☐ `recommendation-event/v1`
- ☐ `trade-thesis/v1`
- ☐ `portfolio-risk/v1`
- ☐ `four-quadrant-risk/v1`
- ☐ `equity-tearsheet/v1`
- ☐ `concentration-xray/v1`
- ☐ `note-audit/v1`

### 2c. Design positions

The undersigned affirms the following design positions:

- ☐ Module 7 (Note Completeness Auditor) is **affirmative and
  non-blocking by design**. It does not substitute for principal review
  under FINRA Rule 3110, and it retains no override logs.
- ☐ Module 2 (Four-Quadrant Risk Framework) does not produce a single
  combined risk score, by design.
- ☐ Module 3 (Portfolio Risk Analyzer) discloses method assumptions in
  the user interface alongside risk metrics.
- ☐ Modules 1, 4, 5 maintain immutable capture timestamps;
  corrections are by supersession rather than in-place edit.

### 2d. Operational positions

The undersigned affirms:

- ☐ The firm's written supervisory procedures ("WSPs") have been or will
  be updated to reference Arctic Edge as a tool in the firm's
  compliance program.
- ☐ The firm's books-and-records program (17a-3 / 17a-4 for BD; 204-2 for
  IA) integrates Arctic Edge output artifacts per `05-recordkeeping.md`.
- ☐ The firm's Reg S-P / NY DFS Part 500 (or equivalent) program covers
  Arctic Edge deployment per `13-data-handling.md`.
- ☐ Annual testing under Rule 206(4)-7 / FINRA 3120 (as applicable)
  covers Arctic Edge per `10-monitoring-and-testing.md`.
- ☐ Advisor and principal training per `09-training-curriculum.md` is
  completed prior to live use.

### 2e. Limitations

The undersigned affirms understanding that this sign-off:

- Does not constitute an opinion on the legality of any specific
  recommendation generated using Arctic Edge.
- Does not transfer compliance responsibility for the firm's program
  from the firm to Arctic Edge.
- Does not waive principal review under FINRA Rule 3110 or other
  applicable supervisory rules.
- Is limited to the version-locked templates attached as Exhibits A and B.

### 2f. Annuity scope (if applicable)

If the firm provides annuity recommendations:

- ☐ Annuity recommendations are **out of scope** for this Phase 1
  sign-off. The firm will execute a separate sign-off for an annuity
  template variant before using Arctic Edge for annuity recommendations.

OR

- ☐ Annuity recommendations are **in scope** under attached **Exhibit C**:
  annuity Reg BI note template variant, tailored under NAIC Model #275
  (or applicable state rule).

## 6. Re-Sign-Off Triggers

This sign-off must be re-executed if any of the following occurs:

- Annual review date passes (12 months from effective date).
- Material SEC or FINRA guidance affects the Reg BI or supervisory
  framework.
- The firm materially changes its product mix, share-class structure,
  or conflict inventory.
- The firm changes its registration status (e.g., adds RIA registration,
  becomes dual-registered).
- A template version is changed (Exhibit A, B, or C).
- A module source change affects regulated logic (Modules 6 or 7).
- An exam finding or enforcement action affects the position taken in
  this sign-off.
