# Variable Annuity Minimum Holding Period

description: Reference for the minimum holding period of a variable annuity, including surrender-charge schedules, free-look windows, tax-deferred withdrawal age rules, 1035-exchange considerations, and rider vesting. Use when advising on liquidity, suitability, surrender decisions, replacement (1035) analysis, or when a client asks "how long do I have to hold this annuity." Triggers on "variable annuity holding period", "surrender period", "surrender charge", "annuity free look", "annuity withdrawal", "1035 exchange", "annuity suitability".

## Quick Answer

A variable annuity does not have one single statutory "minimum holding period." In practice, three different timeframes act as the binding minimums depending on what the client is trying to do:

| Question | Controlling Period | Typical Duration |
|---|---|---|
| When can I cancel for a full refund? | Free-look (state law) | 10 - 30 days from delivery |
| When can I surrender without an insurer penalty? | Surrender-charge schedule (contract) | 5 - 10 years (commonly 7) |
| When can I withdraw earnings without a 10% IRS penalty? | IRC §72(q) early-withdrawal rule | Age 59½ |
| When does a living-benefit rider vest / step up? | Rider terms | 1 - 10 years (commonly waiting period before lifetime income) |
| When can I 1035-exchange the contract? | None statutory; but new surrender clock starts | Immediate (subject to old/new surrender charges) |

The "effective" minimum holding period an advisor should plan around is **the longer of the surrender-charge schedule and age 59½**, unless the client is willing to accept the surrender charge or the 10% tax penalty.

## 1. Free-Look Period (Right to Rescind)

- Set by **state insurance law**, not federal.
- Typically **10 days**, but ranges **10 - 30 days** (e.g., California: 30 days for buyers age 60+; Florida: 21 days; New York: 10 days).
- Begins on **contract delivery**, not issue date.
- During the free-look the owner can return the contract for:
  - **Premium refund** in most states (insurer absorbs market risk), or
  - **Account value refund** in states that allow it (owner absorbs market risk).
- Replacement contracts often have an **extended** free look (commonly 30 days) under state replacement regulations.

Action: confirm the state-specific window on the contract face page; the issuing carrier's prospectus supplement is authoritative.

## 2. Surrender-Charge Schedule (CDSC)

The contingent deferred sales charge schedule is the **contract-level** minimum holding period. Typical patterns:

| Share Class | Surrender Schedule | First-Year Charge | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B-share** (most common) | 6 - 8 years | 7 - 8% | Declining 1% per year |
| **L-share** | 3 - 4 years | 6 - 7% | Shorter lock-up, higher M&E |
| **C-share** | 0 years | 0% | No surrender charge, highest ongoing fees |
| **O-share / bonus** | 8 - 10 years | 8 - 9% | Premium bonus offset by longer surrender |
| **I-share / advisory** | 0 years | 0% | Fee-based, no commission |

Common features that **reduce** the effective minimum:

- **Free withdrawal corridor**: typically **10% of contract value or premium per year**, surrender-charge-free. Unused amounts usually do not accumulate.
- **Nursing home / terminal illness waivers**: surrender charges waived after a qualifying event (often 90 days confinement; terminal illness with < 12 months prognosis).
- **Death**: surrender charges waived on death of owner / annuitant (contract-specific).
- **Annuitization**: charges typically waived if the contract is annuitized after a stated period (often year 1 or 2+).
- **Required Minimum Distributions** (qualified contracts): RMD amounts are typically surrender-charge-free.

Always read the **Charges and Expenses** section of the prospectus — bonus-credit recapture provisions can extend the effective minimum holding period well beyond the stated surrender schedule.

## 3. Tax-Deferred Withdrawal: Age 59½ Rule

Independent of the contract, federal tax law imposes its own holding constraint:

- **IRC §72(q)** (non-qualified annuities) and **§72(t)** (qualified annuities): withdrawals of **earnings** before age 59½ are subject to a **10% additional tax** on top of ordinary income tax.
- Withdrawals are taxed **LIFO** for non-qualified annuities purchased after 8/13/1982 — earnings come out first.
- §72(q) exceptions include: death, disability, substantially-equal-periodic-payments (SEPP / 72(q)(2)(D)), immediate annuity (within 1 year of purchase), and annuitization for life.
- For qualified contracts inside IRAs / 401(k)s, the IRA-level rules (§72(t)) apply, not §72(q).

So even when the surrender schedule has expired, a client under 59½ still faces a tax-driven holding minimum.

## 4. Living-Benefit Rider Waiting Periods

Riders (GLWB, GMIB, GMWB, GMAB) typically impose their own holding periods:

- **GLWB / GMWB**: lifetime withdrawals can usually begin immediately, but the **roll-up rate** (e.g., 5 - 7% deferral bonus) only credits during a **waiting / deferral period**, commonly **10 years or until first withdrawal**.
- **GMIB**: a **waiting period of 7 - 10 years** is generally required before the guaranteed minimum income base can be annuitized.
- **GMAB**: principal protection typically vests after a fixed period (e.g., 10 years).
- **Step-ups / ratchets**: usually annual or every 3 years on contract anniversary; early withdrawals can reset or reduce the benefit base proportionally or dollar-for-dollar.

The rider waiting period is often the **binding** minimum holding period when the rider is the reason the client bought the contract.

## 5. 1035 Exchange Considerations

- **No statutory minimum hold** before doing a 1035 exchange — it can occur immediately after purchase.
- **But**: the old contract's **surrender charge still applies** (unless waived), and the **new contract starts a fresh surrender schedule**.
- FINRA Rule 2330 requires the registered rep to document that the exchange's benefits justify any new surrender period, lost benefits, and increased charges.
- Partial 1035 exchanges: IRS treats the two contracts as a single contract for 180 days (Rev. Proc. 2011-38) — withdrawals from either within that window can disqualify the exchange.

## 6. Suitability & Documentation Framework

When recommending or evaluating a variable annuity, document:

1. **Time horizon**: client must reasonably expect to hold through the surrender period.
2. **Liquidity**: free-withdrawal corridor + emergency reserves outside the annuity cover near-term needs.
3. **Age**: if client is under 59½, plan for tax-deferred holding to at least 59½ unless an exception applies.
4. **Rider purpose**: if a living benefit drove the purchase, the rider's waiting period is the *real* minimum hold.
5. **Replacement**: under FINRA 2330 and state replacement regs, justify why the new surrender clock is in the client's interest.
6. **Share class match**: the surrender schedule should match the client's stated horizon (don't sell a B-share to someone who needs the money in 4 years).

## 7. Output Format

When asked about minimum holding period, respond with:

1. **The three windows** (free-look, surrender, tax) for this specific contract.
2. **Effective minimum** for *this* client (longer of surrender expiry vs. age 59½, or rider waiting period if relevant).
3. **Liquidity available now** (free-look status, free-withdrawal corridor remaining this year, any applicable waivers).
4. **Cost of exiting today** (surrender charge $ + tax & 10% penalty $ + lost rider benefits).

## Important Notes

- Surrender schedules and free-look periods are **contract- and state-specific** — always cite the prospectus and contract data page rather than relying on typical ranges.
- A "no-surrender-charge" or C-share contract still has a tax holding period (59½) and may still have rider waiting periods.
- Bonus-credit recapture can extend the effective holding period beyond the stated surrender schedule.
- This skill provides educational reference only. Specific recommendations require the prospectus, the client's tax bracket, and a suitability review under FINRA 2330 and applicable state rules.
